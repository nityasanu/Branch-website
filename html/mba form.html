10 Key Tips for MBA Admission 2019

Remember these important tips for success in your MBA Admission 2019:






















































  1. MBA Admission through CAT 2018: CAT 2018 is scheduled on November 25, 2018. Submit your Application form online for CAT 2018 before September
    19 for admission to MBA programmes in 20 IIMs, FMS Delhi, IITs and in top PGDM colleges like SPJIMR Mumbai, MDI Gurgaon, IMI New Delhi, IMT ,
    Ghaziabad, TAPMI Manipal among others.

  2. MBA Admission through XAT 2019: XLRI, SPJIMR, XIMB, IMT are some of the best MBA colleges with admissions open through XAT exam score. You
    need to fill up the institute level online application form apart from appearing in XAT 2019. The XAT 2019 exam is scheduled on January 7, 2019 and the last,
    date to apply with application form
    online is November 30.

  3. MBA Admission with NMAT and SNAP 2018: MBA college admission deadlines in NMIMS Mumbai which accepts NMAT score is October 15, 2018 and in
    SIBM Pune, which accepts SNAP score, the MBA application form last date is November 26. There are many similarities in NMAT and SNAP exam and each
    exam score will lead to MBA admission
    2019 in more than 15-20 MBA Colleges.

  4. Entrance Exam Score: It is only minimum eligibility criterion. You should therefore broaden your chances for MBA colleges Admission 2019 by applying to
    more colleges and appearing in more entrance exams.

  5. Be Mobile: Be ready to migrate to large cities like Delhi & Bangalore to get admission in best MBA colleges to get good career opportunities after completing
    the course.

  6. Check the Sectional Required score & Exam Weightage: All the top MBA colleges accepting CAT, XAT scores award different weightage to different sections
    of the entrance exams. You need to prepare and score balanced percentile to get shortlisted at the first stage for MBA admission 2019 in the preferred institute.

  7. Work Experience â€“ an Added Advantage: Your work experience of 1-3 years after graduation can turn the table in your favour in the MBA admission process
    at the MBA colleges. You will be awarded extra points for your supervisory work experience.

  8. Academic Diversity: Non-Engineer category is a preferred lot for MBA Admission 2019 in IIMs and other MBA colleges. All these MBA colleges award
    extra points to Non-Engineer back ground candidates in the range of 4 to 10 percent. If you are from Arts, Commerce background, you stand a better chance
    to get through the MBA Admission process
    at the best MBA colleges.

  9. Prepare your Best and Get ready for GD and Personal Interview: If you are shortlisted by the preferred MBA college, the next is the final selection round.
    This is the most important stage. Your participation and performance in GD-PI can give you an edge over your peers. It is better to prepare for GD round before
    it is too late.

  10. Placement Record: Good Placement over the years is one of the important criteria to finalise the MBA admission. Better to check past placement record and
    average salary of the MBA college.
